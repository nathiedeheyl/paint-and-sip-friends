Get started: The tools you need

Paint brushes are essential tools for any artist, available in various shapes and sizes. Different brushes allow for diverse textures and strokes, from fine detailing to broad strokes. For beginners, a good starter set would include flat brushes for bold strokes and round brushes for finer details. Synthetic brushes are great for beginners because they're durable and affordable.

Suggestion: Start with a set of 5-6 brushes in different shapes and sizes. You can find these at local art supply stores like Bastelbedarf Berlin or Kunst und Spiel. Both offer a wide range of quality brushes suitable for beginners.

Acrylic paint is versatile and quick-drying, making it ideal for beginners. With vibrant colors and smooth application, it’s a favorite among artists. It can be used on various surfaces, including canvas, paper, and wood, offering flexibility in your projects. Acrylic paints are water-based, so they're easy to clean up and are beginner-friendly in terms of blending and layering.

Suggestion: Start with a basic set of 8-12 tubes of acrylic paint in primary colors, so you can mix a wide range of colors. You can find affordable acrylic sets at art supply stores like Gerstaecker or Kunsthaus Berlin, both of which have a good selection for beginners.

A color palette is essential for mixing your paints and achieving the perfect hues. It keeps your colors organized and helps you blend shades for more depth in your artwork. A simple plastic palette is easy to clean and affordable, making it a great choice for beginners. Some artists prefer disposable palettes for convenience.

Suggestion: A plastic palette with wells for mixing colors or a pack of disposable palettes is perfect for starting out. You can purchase these at Kunsthaus Berlin or online, where you can find a variety of options at reasonable prices.

A canvas is your main painting surface, offering a sturdy foundation for your artwork. It comes in various sizes and textures to suit different styles. Pre-stretched canvases are the easiest for beginners to start with, as they’re ready to use right out of the package. If you're looking for more flexibility, canvas boards or paper made specifically for acrylic painting are affordable and beginner-friendly options.

Suggestion: Start with a small set of canvas boards or pre-stretched canvases in 8x10 or 9x12 inches. You can find these at Kunsthaus Berlin or Bastelbedarf Berlin, both offering various sizes and types to suit your needs.

Paint Diamonds and Gemstones

Bring out your inner artist by painting stunning diamonds and gemstones! This project allows you to explore various colors and patterns, creating beautiful decorative pieces that can be used to brighten up any space. You'll be guided through step-by-step instructions to achieve realistic effects, making this project perfect for both beginners and experienced artists alike. By the end, you’ll have a collection of unique, hand-painted stones that can be displayed as art or given as gifts.

Paint Wildflowers on seaside cliff

Immerse yourself in nature by painting vibrant wildflowers set against a stunning seaside backdrop. This project is designed to inspire your creativity while helping you practice blending colors and creating depth in your artwork. You'll learn various techniques for depicting different types of flowers and landscapes. The tutorial offers a clear, six-step approach, ensuring that you can easily follow along and create your own masterpiece that captures the beauty of a coastal scene.

Meetup Times

Our group provides a welcoming space for art enthusiasts to connect. We meet regularly in various locations around Berlin, offering opportunities for local artists to engage in discussions and paint together at 'paint & sip' nights. In addition to in-person events, we host virtual painting sessions to connect with a broader community. Our group trips, such as museum visits, offer unique opportunities to explore art in different settings. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced artist, our meetups foster creativity and connection.
